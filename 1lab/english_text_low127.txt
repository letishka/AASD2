The sun was setting behind the hills, casting long shadows across the empty field. 
A gentle breeze moved through the grass, carrying the smell of dry earth and distant rain. 
In the middle of the field stood a small wooden shed, its paint almost completely worn away by years of wind and sun. 
No one had used it for a long time, but the door was still closed, held shut by a simple iron latch. 
A narrow path led from the shed to a gravel road that disappeared into the forest. 
Somewhere in the trees, a bird called out once, then fell silent. 
It was the kind of evening that made you feel like the world was holding its breath. 
There was no hurry, no noise, no distraction — just the slow fading of light and the quiet certainty that night would follow, as it always did. 
A dog barked far away, and the sound traveled across the open land, thin and clear. 
Then everything was still again, waiting.
